Geo-Referencing

  • SketchUp allows you to geo-reference your building to a specific site. This can be done manually or with Google Earth. The "Get Current View" tool form the Google toolbar in SketchUp goes to Google Earth and brings in the current view. The base image is now geotagged, as is the SketchUp/OpenStudio model (the lattitude and longitude will be set for EnergyPlus). It is important to make sure you set North to up in Google Earth before you get the current view. Otherwise SketchUp will want to change its North direction, which will create conflicts with OpenStudio.

Importing Nearby Buildings as Shading Surfaces

  • A nice use of geo-referencing is to bring in nearby buildings from 3D Warehouse. This isn't very easy to work with right now, as it doesn't sort the buildings by their proximity. An alternative is to go to Google Earth and visually pick the buildings you want. You can click on them and then download the SketchUp or KMZ files.


    • (Credit: David Goldwasser / NREL)

  • The buildings come in correctly located because they are geo-referenced.


    • (Credit: David Goldwasser / NREL)

  • It is easy to copy surfaces of surrounding buildings (you only need surfaces that will provide shading, not the entire building) into a Shading Group. OpenStudio will convert these surfaces to shading surfaces and they will be input to EnergyPlus for determining when and where on our building the sun will shine.
