Matching Surfaces


Drawing the surfaces to be matched

  • In order to match surfaces, OpenStudio requires that their vertices line up exactly. In more technical terms, OpenStudio requires second level space boundaries.
  • In some cases it may help to save your IDF and re-open it before using the surface matching tool as OpenStudio does some geometry clean-up when it opens a file.
  • You should always verify the results of the surface matching operation using the Surface Search Tool, Object Info Dialog, EnergyPlus output, or some other means.
  • Two methods for drawing surfaces in this way are given below.

    • Method 1

    • The first method for drawing surfaces to be matched uses SketchUp's drawing tools and inference locks.
    • Enter into the zone you want to draw your new surface in, but leave the other zones visible.
    • Using the line tool you draw lines that snap to objects in the other zone. SketchUp will indicate this with a pop up note that says "Endpoint Outside Activity".
    • You may have to navigate around the model with the pan and orbit tools to draw all the edges of your surface.
    • If geometry in other zones are in your way, you can temporarily hide them. Select them and then right-click->hide, or go to "hide" under the edit menu.

    Method 2

    • The second method uses "copy" and then "paste in place" vs. manually drawing the second surface.
    • Double click the surface that you want to duplicate in another zone.
    • You should have the face and its bounding edges selected.
    • Shift click on the face to unselect it, but leave the edges selected. The face is unselected to prevent the face-normal of the new copy from being reversed.
    • Now copy the selection, exit out of the current zone and enter into zone that needs the new surface. Once there choose Paste in Place (edit/paste in place). There is no default shortcut set for "paste in place" but there is a separate tutorial that shows you how to create your own shortcuts.

Surface Matching Tool

  • Once your surfaces are drawn, you can use the Surface Matching Tool to match them together.
  • You can match surfaces and sub-surfaces for selected objects or for all objects in the model. Matching two surfaces or sub-surfaces sets the boundary condition of each surface to point to each other and reassigns the default interior construction for that type.
  • You can also use this tool to un-match surfaces. Unmatching two surfaces resets their boundary conditions to be outdoors or ground and reassigns the default exterior construction for that type.
  • You can get a report of the last match or un-match performed in Comma Separated Value (CSV) format to verify the operations that have been performed.


Workflows for Surface Matching

  • You can match surfaces together one at a time as you make them or you can create all surfaces for your model and match them at the end.
  • After matching you can verify correct results using the Surface Search Tool. For example, this can be used to check that no surfaces with outdoor boundary conditions are inside the building and vice versa.
