Using Styles to Show Reversed Faces


  • Every face in SketchUp has a front and a back. Architectural models may not care which way these faces point. But it is critical for energy modeling that the faces are oriented correctly. A face's normal is a line perpendicular to the face going out through the front of the face.
  • In OpenStudio the exterior of a surface is the front face and the interior of the surface is the back of the face.
  • OpenStudio does use a unique front and back face color for surfaces, but it doesn't always jump out at you on a complex model when something is reversed. In particular for windows. You can turn transparency off, but you can also setup a style that is much easier to read.
